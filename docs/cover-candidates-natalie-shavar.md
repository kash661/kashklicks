## Current Cover Assessment

The current cover (16.jpg) is the first kiss at the floral arch. It checks major boxes: romantic moment, both subjects visible, lush white florals framing the couple, and green canopy overhead. It reads well as a wedding image. However, both faces are largely obscured by the kiss angle, and the overall palette skews dark/moody with the heavy green-on-green background. At thumbnail scale, the couple can blend into the busy floral foreground.

## Candidate A (Recommended): 19.jpg

**Description:** Couple embracing in front of the Glenerin Inn's stone facade. Shavar holds Natalie close, her back to camera showing the stunning open-back lace detail of her dress. A copper-leafed tree canopy overhead creates warm tones. The historic stone building with black-framed windows anchors the background.

**Why this works as a cover:**
- Layered composition with foreground interest (couple) + midground (stone architecture) + overhead canopy framing. This matches the "layered compositions with foreground interest" pattern that historically wins.
- Natalie's face is visible and her expression is intimate and confident, looking directly toward camera. Shavar's posture is protective and present.
- The warm copper leaves + stone building create a rich, autumnal color palette that contrasts beautifully with Natalie's white dress. Strong tonal separation at thumbnail scale.
- Showcases the venue (Glenerin Inn) distinctly, which helps prospective clients who are considering the same venue find the portfolio through search.
- The open-back dress detail adds visual interest and fashion-forward appeal.

**Client feeling communicated:** Intimate, elegant, venue-specific. "This photographer captures the romance AND the setting."

**Small-scale readability:** Strong. The white dress against dark stone creates high contrast. Two clear figures, no visual clutter.

## Candidate B: 40.jpg

**Description:** Couple entering the reception through French doors, walking hand-in-hand. Warm interior light spills from the Glenerin's hallway behind them (chandeliers, wood paneling). Natalie is smiling, bouquet in hand. A seated guest on the left edge adds context.

**Why this works as a cover:**
- Cinematic framing: the open French doors create a natural vignette/frame around the couple, drawing the eye inward. This is a strong "layered composition" with architectural foreground elements.
- The warm interior glow behind them creates a luminous, inviting atmosphere that reads as celebratory and joyful.
- Both faces are visible with natural, happy expressions. Natalie is beaming.
- Captures a transitional moment (entering the reception) that tells a story and implies the full wedding day narrative.
- Warm color temperature aligns with the KashKlicks brand palette (gold/warm tones).

**Client feeling communicated:** Joyful arrival, warmth, elegance. "This is what it feels like to walk into your wedding reception."

**Small-scale readability:** Good. The door frame creates a clear focal point. The warm backlight separates the couple from the darker foreground. The couple's silhouettes are immediately legible.

## Candidate C: 18.jpg

**Description:** Recessional shot. Natalie and Shavar walking down the aisle together, just married. Natalie gazes up at Shavar with a glowing smile; he looks back at her. White floral arch behind them, lush green trees overhead. Guests on either side (one visible with phone). Flower petals scattered on the deck.

**Why this works as a cover:**
- Peak emotional moment: the walk back up the aisle as newlyweds. Both faces are clearly visible with genuine, radiant expressions.
- Natural layered depth: couple in foreground, floral arch in midground, tree canopy in background.
- The movement in the image (walking toward camera) creates energy and draws the viewer in.
- The white florals flanking them create symmetry and frame the couple without overwhelming.
- Shows both the ceremony setting and the couple's connection in a single frame.

**Client feeling communicated:** Pure joy, celebration, movement forward together. "They just got married and you can feel it."

**Small-scale readability:** Good. The couple is centered and walking toward camera, creating a clear focal point. The white floral columns on either side frame them. However, slightly busier than Candidate A at very small sizes.
